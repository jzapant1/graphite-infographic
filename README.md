# ORISE Graphite Infographic

## Overview
Under this repository is my submission for the **ORISE Critical Mineral Infographic Undergraduate Competition**. This project focuses on **graphite** as a critical mineral and explores the following: primary uses and applications, current supply chain challenges, potential U.S. sources, technologies or methods used throughout its life cycle, possible material substitutions and strategies for improving manufacturing efficiency

The goal of such a project is to communicate the complex scientific and economic information with regards to graphite through a clear inforgraphic that is accessible to a broad audience.

---

## Why did I choose Graphite?
Graphite, under the U.S. Department of Energy, is classified as a **Non-elemental Critical Material** due to its liabilities in the supply chain despite its importance in a multitude of industries. It is especially vital for:

- **Lithium-ion batteries** within electric vehicles and smartphones as an anode
- **Steel production** (as a component in refractories and lubricants)
- **Electronics and advanced materials** such as brake linings, foils, and powdered metals

In spite of the key applications that graphite has, its supply is constrained and geographically concentrated, posing potential risks in the global market.

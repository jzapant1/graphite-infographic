PRIMARY USES
Where Graphite Goes

Applications Driving Demand

With EVs and energy storage already claiming 43% of the market, battery production ramping up everywhere will cause a global shift in graphite trade through the 2030s.

About 22% of graphite goes into refractories where it is one of the few materials on Earth that can face 3,000°C heat just fine.

Because of its unique structure, graphite becomes a natural choice for lubricant where it stays friction-free in extreme environments where even grease would fail.

With 8% of the market is dedicated to expandable graphite, the material plays a critical role in keeping buildings safe from fire and overheating.
